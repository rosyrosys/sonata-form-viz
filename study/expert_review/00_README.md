# Expert Review — Preliminary Validation Package

A 2-week, 2-expert qualitative validation protocol for the
Sonata Form Visualization tool. Used as a *preliminary expert review*
preceding the planned randomized controlled trial. Designed to be
included in §6 of the TISMIR manuscript as empirical validation
before the RCT.

## Why this exists

The manuscript (without this data) presents system design and
analytical case study without empirical evaluation. A TISMIR
reviewer may legitimately ask whether the tool's pedagogical claims
have any empirical grounding. This package provides a methodologically
sound, IRB-light validation that:

- Uses standard instruments (Brooke 1996 SUS) translated to Korean.
- Treats experts as *evaluators*, not *human subjects* — most Korean
  university IRBs deem this exempt or qualify for simplified review.
- Produces both quantitative (SUS) and qualitative (thematic analysis
  of semi-structured interviews) evidence within 2 weeks.

## Methodology in one paragraph

Two music education experts (1 music-theory pedagogue, 1
music-education researcher / practitioner) independently complete a
1-hour structured session: (a) self-exploration of the tool, (b)
guided execution of the §5 classroom walk-through as if teaching, (c)
SUS administration, (d) semi-structured interview on pedagogical
fidelity, affordance utility, and risks of student misconception.
SUS scores are reported individually and as a mean; interview
transcripts are coded inductively (Braun & Clarke 2006 thematic
analysis) by the researcher; emergent themes are reported with
illustrative quotes.

## What success looks like

For this preliminary stage, "success" is *not* a SUS > 80 or any
particular target score. It is:

1. Two SUS scores (any values) with documented context.
2. 4–6 inductively-identified themes from the interviews, each with
   at least one expert quote.
3. A short list of *design recommendations* the experts propose,
   which can either be implemented before submission or recorded as
   future-work items.

A SUS in the 60–80 range is consistent with "marginal usability"
which is still publishable — the value is the *qualitative*
findings and the demonstration that an expert pre-validation
informed the planned RCT design.

## Ethics — quick note

This is **expert evaluation**, not learner/student research. Korean
university IRBs generally treat this as outside the scope of
human-subjects research (or qualified for simplified review) because:

- Experts are professionals providing professional judgment, not
  research subjects.
- No personally identifying data is collected beyond name +
  institutional affiliation (used only in acknowledgments with
  permission).
- The "intervention" is evaluating a tool, not the experts
  themselves.

See `07_irb_exemption_note.md` for the full justification and an
optional IRB-exemption application template.
