## 1. Purpose

This study quantitatively evaluates whether a web-based learning tool
that simultaneously displays score, audio, and formal analysis improves
undergraduate students' ability to identify *sonata form*. The case
work is W. A. Mozart's Piano Sonata in C Major, K. 545, Movement I.

## 2. Procedure

If you agree to participate, you will complete a single ~60-minute
session containing the following:

1. Pretest survey on demographics and music background (5 min).
2. Listen to twelve 30-second excerpts and select a formal label for
   each (10 min).
3. Use the tool to explore K. 545 Mvt. I freely (30 min).
4. Posttest labeling on twelve different excerpts (10 min).
5. One free-response item + ten-item System Usability Scale (10 min).

Headphone volume is participant-controlled; ≤ 70 dB is recommended.

## 3. Eligibility

- Age 18 or older.
- Currently enrolled, undergraduate years 2–4.
- Music-related major (music education, musicology, applied music,
  etc.).
- Self-reported normal hearing.

## 4. Risks and Discomforts

Risks are *minimal* and equivalent to everyday music listening and
survey completion. You may stop at any moment if you feel discomfort.

## 5. Benefits

- Personal: increased ability to recognize sonata form through use of
  the tool.
- Compensation: a **book voucher worth approximately KRW 50,000** (or
  local equivalent) at the end of the session.

## 6. Anonymity and Data Handling

- Your responses are recorded under an **anonymous code (e.g., P-007)**.
- Your name, student number, and email are stored on the consent form
  only and **kept separate** from the analysis dataset.
- All study materials are retained for five years and then permanently
  destroyed.
- Anonymized response data may be released after publication on OSF.io
  under CC BY 4.0; individual identification is not possible.

## 7. Voluntariness

- Participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time
  *without penalty*.
- Upon withdrawal your data is destroyed (already-anonymized data
  already merged into aggregate statistics may not be technically
  retrievable; the investigators will explain this at debrief).

## 8. Use of Results

Results will be used in academic publications and conference
presentations. No individual response will be released in identifiable
form.
